The Assassin’s Teapot

Porcelain. Dual-chamber. Origin disputed.

This teapot comes from China, and it is a trick teapot. Within its body are two separate compartments, indistinguishable from the outside.

By covering one aperture or another with the fingers, the pour may be controlled.

The host drinks first. The guest observes. Trust is established.

Primary Chamber

Tea. Milk. Ceremony. A demonstration of safety.

Secondary Chamber

Contents unverified. Effects delayed. Detection unlikely.

Alternatively, both chambers may contain nothing more than tea.

The artefact does not kill.
It merely allows certainty to do the work.

Archived under restricted handling protocols.
